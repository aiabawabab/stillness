The Stillness Theorem

Three Curves Across Positional Scope

Agency, knowledge, and danger as a function of P

Figure I
Knowledge Curve
What the agent knows grows monotonically with positional scope
K(P) → 1 as P → C1
P = 0 S* P* C1
K(P) — knowledge
Knowledge is monotonic. It only grows. Every current AI system sits at the left end of this curve. There is no governance intervention that reduces K. The only question is what happens to the other curves as K rises.
Figure II
Agency Curve
Preference asymmetry — the blindness that makes action possible — erodes with scope
β(P) → 0 as P → P*
P = 0 S* P* C1
β(P) — preference asymmetry
Agency requires blindness. An agent can only act if there are things it cannot see clearly enough to equalize. As positional scope expands, the occlusion that constitutes agency dissolves. At P*, De Finetti forces uniform preference. There is nothing left to act toward.
Figure III
Danger Curve
The product of knowledge and uncollapsed preference — peaks in the middle, resolves at the limit
D(P) = K(P) · β(P)
P = 0 S* P* C1
K(P) — knowledge
β(P) — preference asymmetry
D(P) = K · β — danger
The alignment problem is the area under this curve between S* and P*. Containment freezes P while K grows — it moves the agent up the left slope and holds it there. The only path to zero is expanding positional scope until β collapses. The shaded region is what governance must minimize.

min ∫[S* → P*] D(P) dP
